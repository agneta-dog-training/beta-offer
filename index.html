Why virtual works

Virtual might not be what you'd expect for dog behaviour work, but for most cases it's actually the better format.

  • Your dog gets to be themselves — A trainer's presence can change how your dog behaves in the moment. Virtually, I see their real, everyday behaviour, which gives us a much more accurate picture to work from.
  • You can focus on what matters — In person, you're often managing your dog while also trying to absorb new information. Virtually, you can be more present in the conversation and the learning.
  • Video shows us what's actually happening — Filming between sessions is one of the most powerful tools we have. We can slow things down, catch subtle body language, and pinpoint exactly what to adjust. Virtual sessions are a great platform for this.
  • You're the one living with your dog — My job is to teach you how to work through your dog's behaviour challenges so that the progress you make together sticks in real life. It matters less that I can get your dog to do something, and more that you can.
